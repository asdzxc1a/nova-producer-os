## Executive Summary

*The Kill List* is a tightly wound psychological thriller centered on Elena Voss, a disgraced intelligence analyst who discovers her own name on a classified assassination ledger. The script leverages paranoia, institutional decay, and moral ambiguity to create sustained tension without relying on expensive set pieces.

## Script Assessment

### Strengths
- **Contained locations:** 70% of the story unfolds in a single Berlin safe-house, minimizing production footprint.
- **Two-hander potential:** The central relationship between Elena and the handler sent to kill her offers rich dramatic territory for two strong leads.
- **Third-act reversal:** The reveal that Elena authored the list herself (in a dissociative state) provides a memorable, conversation-worthy ending.

### Risks
- **Dialogue density:** Some scenes run 4+ pages of rapid-fire exposition. A polish pass is recommended before casting.
- **Cold open ambiguity:** The opening murder may read as genre-generic. Consider starting in medias res with the list discovery.
